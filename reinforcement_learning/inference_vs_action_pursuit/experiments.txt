WHAT TO LOOK FOR

Across all 38 runs:
  • α flip: Inference density should rise as α increases from -2 to +2.
            At α<0 (far cells clear): infer when far. At α>0 (far cells noisy): infer when close.
  • σ₀ ramp: Higher noise → more total inference, but spatial pattern unchanged by σ₀ alone.
  • p_drift effect: Higher drift → inference density rises uniformly (drift adds temporal pressure).
  • α=0 sanity: At α=0, inference density should be approximately flat vs. distance (only uncertainty matters, not location).
  • GROUP 2 baseline: Should show clean α effect without drift confound. Use to validate GROUP 1 patterns.
